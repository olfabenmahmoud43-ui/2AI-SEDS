About the Workshop

The advanced Artificial Intelligence (AI) that has been included into logic engineering has significantly changed how algorithms are developed, tested, and maintained. Deep Learning (DL) makes it possible to automatically extract complex expressions from large amounts of code, making tasks like anomaly detection, fault prediction, and automatic code generation easier. On the other hand, Reinforcement Learning (RL) provides an adaptive learning framework that enables the optimization of development, resource allocation, or software service orchestration processes based on dynamic environmental returns.

Furthermore, semantic analysis of source code, automatic documentation, and comprehension of software behavior are all aided by Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques, such as those incorporated into intelligent programming assistants. When combined, these methods open the door to a new era of intelligent software engineering marked by more autonomous, effective, and self-adaptive systems.

The goal of the AIAI-SEDS 2026 workshop is to bring together researchers and practitioners to discuss developments, challenges, and emerging trends in the fields of artificial intelligence applied to distributed and logical systems. The workshop places special emphasis on contemporary methods of DL, RL, and NLP, which are crucial to the modeling, semantic reasoning, and dynamic adaptation of complex logical systems.

Call for Papers (CFP)

We welcome original research and experience papers that explore recent advances in Deep Learning (DL), Reinforcement Learning (RL), and Natural Language Processing (NLP) applied to software engineering and distributed intelligent systems.

Publication & Indexation

Accepted papers will be included in the conference proceedings of Lecture Notes in Data Engineering and Communication Technologies (LNDECT) published by Springer. Indexing: the LNDECT series is indexed by EI and SCOPUS.

Topics of Interest
  • AI for software design, testing, and maintenance
  • Deep learning architectures for code analysis
  • Reinforcement learning for software optimization and orchestration
  • Natural language processing for code understanding and documentation
  • Semantic reasoning and intelligent decision-making in software systems
  • Federated and distributed learning frameworks
  • Edge-cloud collaboration and AI orchestration
  • Autonomous and adaptive service management
  • Human-centric AI in software engineering
  • Security, privacy, and trust in AI-based software systems
  • Explainable and ethical AI in distributed software contexts
